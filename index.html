Surface Level — The D-Shape City
Ground · 0m
Central Spires on lake island. Parks and waterways flanking. Residential clusters in mid-ring. Agricultural outer arc split: aquaculture (east/bay-facing), eco farms (north), pasture (west), orchards (south). Forest buffer merging into native coastal dark forest at perimeter.
↑ Tidal canal connects central lake to bay. Bay mouth sealed by tidal barrage.
Tidal Energy Infrastructure
Bay · 0–20m depth
Tidal barrage generators spanning bay entrance. Tidal stream turbines in canal. Sluice gates controlling tidal flow into city waterway system. Predictable 9-day neap/spring cycle allows precise generation scheduling.
↑ New addition. Potentially reduces nuclear load significantly — tidal is primary, nuclear becomes backup/industrial.
Utility & Logistics
0–30m depth
Water treatment, waste recycling, freight tunnels, data centers. Tidal canal infrastructure integrated at this level where it passes under the city footprint.
Light Industry
30–150m depth
Food production, electronics fabrication, workshops. Aquaculture processing relocated here from surface — bay catch processed underground, minimizing surface industrial footprint.
↑ Aquaculture processing added.
Heavy Industry
150–300m depth
Metal recycling, chemical plants. Energy storage potentially downsized — tidal power is predictable enough that large buffer storage is less critical than with solar or wind.
↑ Energy storage tier reduced due to tidal reliability.
Nuclear Complex
300–500m depth
SMR reactors now function as backup and heavy industrial load supply rather than primary power. Fuel reprocessing and waste storage unchanged. KOI's greater geological activity makes deep placement here especially important.
↑ Demoted from primary to backup role. Consider reducing to 2 SMRs from 3.
Deep Reserve Level
500m+ depth
Archives, seed vaults, secure supply bunkers, emergency habitats. Unchanged from original design. At this depth fully isolated from surface geological events — appropriate for KOI's more active tectonics.
